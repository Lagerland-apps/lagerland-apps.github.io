On-device AI · Medical-record depth · No account

A PetNoter alternative
built around scanning the medical record.

PetNoter is a friendly all-in-one pet-care tracker — daily walks, meals, tasks, and memories alongside vaccinations, weight, and documents, free on iPhone and Android with an optional lifetime pass. Pawza overlaps on records and the vet PDF, but it's pointed at a different center of gravity: the medical record, built with on-device AI that reads your vet documents and fills them in, kept entirely on your device with no account. If daily-care logging is your main need, PetNoter is great; if it's the medical record and privacy, Pawza is the alternative.

How does Pawza compare to PetNoter?

They share a vet PDF and vaccination tracking — and diverge from there.

Center of gravity
Pawza: The medical record — vaccinations, meds, weight, visits — built with AI scanning.

PetNoter: All-in-one daily care — walks, meals, tasks, memories — plus records.
Getting records in
Pawza: Snap a document; on-device AI fills it in.

PetNoter: Manual entry, with document storage.
Privacy & storage
Pawza: On-device with optional private iCloud sync; no account.

PetNoter: Account-based sync across iPhone and Android.
Vet PDF export
Pawza: Yes — full-history PDF (Pro).

PetNoter: Yes — PDF health report export.
Platforms
Pawza: iPhone and iPad (universal). Apple-only.

PetNoter: iPhone and Android.
Pricing
Pawza: Free for one pet; Pro $2.99/mo, $19.99/yr, or $39.99 once (Lifetime).

PetNoter: Free, with an optional lifetime pass.

Questions

FAQ

What is the best PetNoter alternative for iPhone?
Pawza, if your priority is the medical record rather than daily-care logging. Both track vaccinations and weight and export a vet PDF, but Pawza adds on-device AI document scanning and keeps everything on your device with no account. PetNoter is cross-platform and stronger on daily care like walks, meals, and tasks.
Does Pawza export a vet PDF like PetNoter?
Yes. Both export a vet-visit PDF. In Pawza, the PDF generator (part of Pro) turns a pet's full history — vaccinations, medications, weight, visits, and notes — into a clean, printable document to hand the vet or keep for boarding and travel.
Can Pawza scan vet documents to fill in records?
Yes — it's the headline feature and the main thing PetNoter doesn't do. Photograph a certificate, lab result, or prescription and Pawza reads it on-device and fills in the fields for you to confirm. Documents are never uploaded.
Is Pawza more private than PetNoter?
Pawza keeps records on your device with optional private iCloud sync, needs no account, scans on-device, and has no third-party tracking. For on-device, account-free privacy, Pawza is the more private design.
How much does Pawza cost compared to PetNoter?
PetNoter is free on iOS and Android with an optional lifetime pass. Pawza is free for one pet; Pro is $2.99/month or $19.99/year (each with a 7-day free trial), or $39.99 once as a Lifetime purchase with Family Sharing.
