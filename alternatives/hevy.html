How does LiftLog compare to Hevy?

Different pricing, similar core. Here's the line.

Pricing model
LiftLog: Free 7-day trial (no card), then pay-what-you-can one-time $9.99-$39.99.

Hevy: Freemium; Hevy Pro on subscription for advanced features.
Programs
LiftLog: Periodized program templates; haptic-confirmed sets; PR detection.

Hevy: Routines library, share-with-friends programs, large community templates.
Social
LiftLog: None. Solo training tool.

Hevy: Friends, social feed, workout sharing.
Privacy / account
LiftLog: No account. See the Privacy Manifest.

Hevy: Account required for sync and social.

Who should pick LiftLog vs Hevy?

Pick LiftLog if…
  • You train solo and don't need a social feed
  • You're tired of fitness subscriptions and want to pay once
  • You care about design and haptic feel during a set
  • You run periodized strength cycles and want clean 1RM analytics
  • You'd rather your workout history not live on someone else's server
Stick with Hevy if…
  • You actively use the social / friends / sharing features
  • You rely on the community routines library
  • You have years of Hevy history you don't want to migrate
  • You're already happy with the Pro subscription

Is LiftLog a one-time purchase?
Yes. After a free 7-day trial with no card required, you pick a one-time price between $9.99 and $39.99. There is no recurring charge and no Pro tier locked behind a subscription.
How is LiftLog different from GymLogger X?
LiftLog and GymLogger X are both Lagerland apps but solve slightly different problems. GymLogger X is a fast, minimalist tracker with Apple Watch as the primary surface and 1,500+ exercises. LiftLog is design-led, focused on periodized strength programs, 1RM analytics, and haptic-confirmed lifts — pick whichever workflow fits your training style.
Does LiftLog track me?
No. LiftLog has no third-party tracking, no advertising SDKs, and no required account. Workout data stays on the device and syncs through your own iCloud account if you enable it.
