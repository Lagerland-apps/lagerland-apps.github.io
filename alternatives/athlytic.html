How does Observa compare to Athlytic?

Different jobs. Observa is an interpretation layer; Athlytic is a scoring layer. Run both — or pick the one that answers the question you actually have.

What it does
Observa: Reads Apple Health data, builds a 60-day personal baseline for each metric, surfaces single-variable (free) and multi-variable (Pro) correlations as plain-English sentences with sample size and confidence.

Athlytic: Synthesises HRV, RHR, sleep, and strain into a daily recovery percentage and effort score in the WHOOP visual style.
What you ask it
Observa: "Which of my habits is consistently moving my recovery?" "Why was this week different?" "Is my training load sustainable?"

Athlytic: "How recovered am I today?" "What's my strain score?" "Should I train hard or take it easy?"
Data handling
Observa: Fully on-device, no server, no account, no analytics SDKs. Apple's App Privacy nutrition label lists zero data collected.

Athlytic: Reads Apple Health; check the current App Store privacy disclosures for any cloud or analytics.
Pricing
Observa: Free forever, Pro from $2.99/mo or $49.99 lifetime.

Athlytic: Typically a one-time purchase or annual subscription via the App Store.

When to pick Observa over Athlytic

  • You already understand what a recovery score is — you want to know what's consistently moving it for you specifically
  • You care about your personal HRV (SDNN) and sleep-efficiency baseline more than a 0–100 dashboard
  • You want named multi-variable correlations (training load × caffeine timing × sleep) with sample size and confidence — not a single number
  • You want every byte of computation to happen on your device, with App Privacy nutrition label proof
  • You'd rather pay $49.99 once than subscribe annually for a score you already understand

When to keep Athlytic instead

  • You want a single daily score and don't need the underlying reasoning
  • You like the WHOOP-style visualisation and recovery percentage as your primary signal
  • You're a high-volume trainee who relies on a strain score for daily programming decisions

FAQ

What is the best Athlytic alternative?
Observa is the closest match if what you want is interpretation rather than a single score. It reads the same Apple Health data — HRV (SDNN), RHR, sleep stages, ECG, workouts — and explains in plain English which habits are consistently moving each metric against your personal 60-day baseline. Free, fully on-device, no account.
Does Observa replace the Athlytic recovery score?
Not as a number. Observa doesn't compute a single 0–100 percentage. It scores each metric independently against your own baseline and tells you what's moving it. If you only want a daily dashboard score, Athlytic is the better fit; if you want to understand why that score changed, Observa is built for that.
Is Observa more private than Athlytic?
Yes — Observa runs entirely on-device with no servers, no account, and no third-party analytics. Apple's App Privacy nutrition label lists Observa as collecting zero data. Athlytic has historically been a privacy-friendly app, but check its current App Store disclosure for any cloud sync or analytics.
Can I use Observa and Athlytic together?
Yes. Both read from the same Apple Health pool, so running them side-by-side gives you Athlytic's daily score and Observa's named correlations behind it.
