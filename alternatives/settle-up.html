How does RightSplit compare to Settle Up?

Different jobs entirely — Settle Up is a ledger; RightSplit closes one bill.

Scope
RightSplit: One bill, settled now, closed out.

Settle Up: Ongoing group ledger across many expenses.
Receipt OCR
RightSplit: On-device Apple Vision framework reads every line item — no upload, no server.

Settle Up: Manual expense entry; no receipt OCR as a core feature.
Tip math
RightSplit: Proportional by subtotal share — documented openly, fair rounding.

Settle Up: Equal-split convention; tip handled as a line item.
Pricing
RightSplit: Free + $1.99/yr or $7.99 lifetime (one-time).

Settle Up: Free + Pro tier for advanced features.

Who should pick RightSplit vs Settle Up?

Pick RightSplit if…
  • Your splits are mostly single bills, not running tabs
  • You'd rather scan the receipt than type each item
  • You want documented OCR + tip math transparency
  • You prefer a lifetime price ($7.99 once) to a subscription
  • You don't want a group ledger sitting open in your phone
Stick with Settle Up if…
  • You're tracking shared expenses across weeks or months
  • You and your group rely on the running-balance view
  • You need multi-currency long-term ledger features
  • Your group is already onboarded onto Settle Up

FAQ

What is the best Settle Up alternative for one-off bills?
RightSplit is a privacy-first iPhone alternative to Settle Up when the bill in front of you is the entire transaction — not a line in a running group ledger. Receipt OCR runs on-device with Apple's Vision framework; item-by-item split with proportional tip; share each total via iMessage or WhatsApp. Free, with Pro at $1.99/year or $7.99 lifetime.
Does RightSplit track group balances over time?
No — by design. RightSplit settles one bill at a time and closes it out. If you want a running group balance across many transactions and many months, Settle Up's ledger model is the right fit. RightSplit is what you reach for at the table when the bill comes.
Does RightSplit need an account like Settle Up?
No. Nobody needs an account. You enter names locally and share each person's total via the iOS share sheet — Messages, WhatsApp, or any other app.
