Questions

FAQ

What is the best Shogi Wars alternative for training?
Shogiful is the closest alternative for the player who wants to improve rather than compete. Shogi Wars is built around live online matchmaking with kyu/dan ranking; Shogiful is built around the improvement loop — every game analyzed on-device by YaneuraOu, mistakes explained in plain language, and training generated from your own weaknesses. Fully offline, no account, on iPhone, iPad, and Mac.
Can I play Shogiful without reading kanji?
Yes — this is one of Shogiful's core features. The Western piece set uses symbolic icons with movement indicators so the board is readable from your first game, and the app introduces the kanji characters progressively as you train.
Does Shogiful have online play like Shogi Wars?
No — deliberately. Shogiful is a single-player training product with 10 AI opponents across four tiers. For ranked human play, Shogi Wars remains the right tool; Shogiful is designed to make you better between those games.
How much does Shogiful cost compared to Shogi Wars?
Shogiful is free with all 10 opponents, 2 analyses per day, and 3 puzzles per day. Premium is $1.99/month, $9.99/year, or $19.99 once for lifetime. Shogi Wars is free for limited daily games with a subscription for unlimited play and paid AI-assist features.
Does Shogiful analyze my games?
Yes — every game. YaneuraOu reviews every move on-device, and each significant mistake is classified into shogi-specific categories — missed drop tactics, wrong promotions, castle weaknesses, missed tsume — and explained in plain language.
Can I use both Shogi Wars and Shogiful?
Yes, and that's the most common pattern for improving players. Shogi Wars gives you human opposition and an official rank; Shogiful gives you the analysis and training loop that turns those games into improvement. KIF import from Shogi Wars game records is under consideration on Shogiful's roadmap.
