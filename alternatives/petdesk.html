A PetDesk alternative
that's your record, not your clinic's.

PetDesk is handed to you by a participating veterinary clinic, and it's good at what it does: booking appointments, requesting refills, and surfacing the reminders your vet sends. But its value is tied to your clinic using PetDesk. Pawza is the opposite: a private record you own, that works with any vet or none. Snap a vet document and on-device AI fills it in, keep it all on your device with no account, and export a clean PDF for any visit. Use Pawza alongside PetDesk, or instead of it if your clinic isn't on PetDesk.

How does Pawza compare to PetDesk?

They overlap on reminders and records, but they're built for different jobs.

What it's for
Pawza: Your own private medical record — vaccinations, meds, weight, visits, photos — built with AI scanning.

PetDesk: A bridge to your clinic — appointments, refills, and clinic-sent reminders.
Clinic dependency
Pawza: None. Works with any vet, any country, and survives switching clinics.

PetDesk: Core features require your clinic to use PetDesk's software.
Getting records in
Pawza: Snap a document and on-device AI fills in the fields you confirm.

PetDesk: Records flow from the participating clinic; you don't build them yourself.
Privacy & storage
Pawza: On-device with optional private iCloud sync. No account.

PetDesk: Account-based service connected to your clinic, data held in its cloud.
Booking & refills
Pawza: Not offered — it's a records app, not a clinic portal.

PetDesk: Its strength — request appointments and prescription refills directly.
Pricing
Pawza: Free for one pet; Pro $2.99/mo, $19.99/yr, or $39.99 once (Lifetime).

PetDesk: Free to owners; your clinic pays for the platform.

Questions

FAQ

What is the best PetDesk alternative for keeping my own records?
Pawza, if your goal is a record you own independently of any clinic. PetDesk centers on booking, refills, and clinic-sent reminders; Pawza is your own private record built with AI scanning, stored on-device with no account, and works with any vet. Many owners use both.
Does PetDesk work if my vet doesn't use it?
PetDesk's core features depend on your clinic using PetDesk's software. If your clinic isn't on it, much of that value isn't available. Pawza has no such dependency — it's your own record and works the same regardless of which vet you see.
Is my data more private in Pawza than in PetDesk?
Pawza keeps records on your device with optional private iCloud sync and no account — there's no Pawza server holding your data. PetDesk is an account-based service connected to your clinic, with data in its cloud. For on-device privacy, Pawza is the more private design.
Can Pawza book appointments or request refills like PetDesk?
No — that's the honest dividing line. Booking and refills need a live connection to your clinic's systems, which is what PetDesk provides. Pawza is a records app, not a clinic portal. If your vet uses PetDesk, keep it for booking and use Pawza as your private record alongside it.
How much does Pawza cost compared to PetDesk?
PetDesk is free to owners because clinics pay for it. Pawza is free for one pet; Pro is $2.99/month or $19.99/year (each with a 7-day free trial), or $39.99 once as a Lifetime purchase with Family Sharing.
