When should I pick Forest instead?

Honest answer — pick Forest if any of these matter more than an enforced, movement-gated block:

  • You love the gamification — growing trees, unlocking species, building a forest is genuinely motivating for you.
  • You want your focus time to fund real tree planting. Forest's partner program has planted well over a million real trees; EarnLock does nothing like it.
  • You need cross-platform — Forest runs on iOS, Android, and as a browser extension. EarnLock is iOS + Apple Watch only.
  • You want group focus sessions with friends, and you don't mind an account.
  • A gentle nudge works for you and you don't want a hard system-level shield.

EarnLock is built for a different person: someone who found the tree easy to abandon and wants the unlock cost to be something their body actually pays — enforced by Apple's Family Controls, proven by Apple Health.

What is the best alternative to Forest?

EarnLock instead uses Apple's Family Controls to actually shield your chosen apps until you hit a real daily activity goal (steps, active minutes, or active energy) measured by Apple Health. The custom shield paints a live progress ring with the exact count remaining on every blocked app. Free to start; $19.99 lifetime (one-time). No account, no analytics SDK, no server.
How is EarnLock different from Forest?
Forest is a gamified focus timer: a tree grows while you stay off the phone and withers if you leave. The accountability is emotional, not enforced — nothing physically stops you from leaving. EarnLock enforces a real block through Apple's Family Controls and unlocks only when your body hits a daily activity goal measured by Apple Health. EarnLock also makes the Apple Watch a first-class surface so the count runs without the iPhone. Forest plants real trees and works cross-platform; EarnLock is iOS and Apple Watch only.
Is EarnLock cheaper than Forest?
Not on the upfront price — Forest is roughly a $3.99 one-time purchase on iOS and is free to start, which is hard to beat. EarnLock is also free to start, then $19.99 lifetime (one-time) or $1.99/mo / $9.99/yr with a 7-day trial. The reason to pay for EarnLock isn't a lower price than Forest; it's the harder, movement-gated block, the live progress shield, the Apple Watch surface, and zero account or tracking.
Does Forest actually block apps?
Forest's core mechanic is a focus timer with a virtual tree that dies if you leave the app; it is designed as a motivational nudge rather than a hard system-level block. EarnLock uses Apple's Family Controls framework to genuinely shield the apps you pick across the whole device, so they cannot be opened until your daily activity goal is met (or you spend your one math-gated emergency unlock).
Is EarnLock private?
Yes. EarnLock has no account, no server, no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, and no third-party SDKs of any kind. HealthKit data is read on-device only and never transmitted off the device. EarnLock can block any iOS app or category via Apple's Family Controls picker — Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, Reddit, games, anything you choose, device-wide. Forest's focus sessions encourage you to stay off the phone entirely rather than shielding a specific selection at the system level. If your goal is "these specific apps stay locked until I move," EarnLock is the more direct fit.
