FAQ

Do I need an Apple Watch, or does Observa work with Oura, Garmin, and Fitbit?
No Apple Watch required. Observa reads Apple Health and interprets whatever is in it — Oura, Whoop, Garmin, or Fitbit data all work (Garmin/Fitbit usually via a sync app like Health Sync). Runs on iPhone and iPad.
What is the best HRV4Training alternative?
Observa, for people who want passive interpretation instead of a daily guided reading. It reads the HRV, RHR, and sleep already in your Apple Health — from any device that writes there — and surfaces which habits move each metric against your baseline.
Do I have to take a daily reading with Observa like HRV4Training?
No — Observa is passive, reading what your Apple Watch already records. HRV4Training's deliberate morning reading is strong if you like that discipline; Observa works without adding a daily ritual.
Does Observa give training advice like HRV4Training?
Differently. HRV4Training gives explicit train/easy guidance. Observa interprets recovery, sleep, and energy — which habits move your metrics, with confidence — rather than a daily verdict. Want prescriptive readiness? HRV4Training. Want to understand patterns? Observa.
Is Observa private like HRV4Training?
Both are privacy-respecting. Observa runs entirely on-device with no account; App Privacy label lists zero data collected. HRV4Training is also research-grounded and privacy-conscious.
How does Observa's pricing compare to HRV4Training?
Observa is free at core, Pro from $2.99/month or $49.99 lifetime. HRV4Training is typically ~$9.99 one-time with an optional Pro/Coach subscription. Both avoid wearable hardware costs.
Can I use both Observa and HRV4Training?
Yes — take your morning reading in HRV4Training for readiness, and let Observa interpret the broader Apple Health picture passively.
