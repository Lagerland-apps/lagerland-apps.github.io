App Store Connect on iPhone · Read-only · Zero tracking

Looking for an
AppStats alternative?

AppStats is a well-built read-only App Store Connect dashboard for the same audience AppMeta Pulse serves — indie iOS developers and small studios who want revenue, subscription, and download numbers on their phone without opening a browser. Both use Apple's official App Store Connect API. Both keep credentials on-device. If AppStats' daily notifications and home-screen widgets are what you want, keep it. How does AppMeta Pulse compare to AppStats?

Honest framing: AppStats is a serious app with a clear point of view, and on two specific features it is currently the stronger product. The two apps trade off differently. Here is where the differences matter in practice.

Price
AppMeta Pulse: $0.99/month · $8.99/year · $17.99 lifetime.

AppStats: $2.99/month · $19.99/year · $49.99 lifetime.

AppMeta Pulse is roughly 67% cheaper monthly and 64% cheaper at the lifetime tier.
Privacy label — what each app declares to Apple
AppMeta Pulse: Privacy label declares zero data collection. No usage data, no diagnostics, no third-party SDKs.

AppStats: Privacy label declares usage data and diagnostics collection (not linked to identity, per the listing).

Both are read-only against App Store Connect; the difference is in what the apps themselves do after fetching the data.
Notifications and widgets
AppMeta Pulse: On-demand glance — open the app, see the numbers. Quieter product surface.

AppStats: Dedicated daily morning notifications with customisable preferences. Multiple home-screen widget sizes showing downloads, revenue, and top products with background updates.

If notifications and widgets are how you actually check your numbers, AppStats wins this category today.
Vocabulary and reporting
AppMeta Pulse: Uses Apple's own terms throughout — proceeds (after commission and VAT), sales (gross), units, refunds, active subscriptions, trial conversions. Direct mapping to ASC's Sales and Trends + Subscription reports.

AppStats: Similar coverage. Multi-currency toggle (USD/EUR) where AppMeta Pulse displays Apple's reported currencies.
Platforms
AppMeta Pulse: iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Vision Pro.

AppStats: iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Mac, Apple Vision.

Equivalent platform coverage on both sides.
Authentication and credentials
AppMeta Pulse: App Store Connect API key (.p8 + key ID) in system Keychain. Read-only scope only.

AppStats: Same model — API key on-device. Read-only scope.

Both apps are credential-equivalent. Neither can write to your App Store Connect account.

Who should pick AppMeta Pulse vs AppStats?

Pick AppMeta Pulse if…
  • You want the cheapest credible App Store Connect dashboard on iPhone
  • You want a privacy label that declares zero data collection — not "minimal" or "anonymous"
  • You check your numbers on your own schedule rather than via notifications
  • You ship multiple apps and want a calm portfolio view without push interruptions
  • You prefer Apple's official vocabulary (proceeds, units, sales) over a third-party rebrand
Stick with AppStats if…
  • Daily morning revenue notifications are how you actually check your business
  • You use home-screen widgets heavily and want multiple sizes with background updates
  • You specifically need a multi-currency toggle (USD ↔ EUR) baked in
  • You have months of AppStats history you don't want to migrate

FAQ

What is the best alternative to AppStats for iPhone?
AppMeta Pulse is a read-only App Store Connect dashboard built for the same indie iOS developer audience. Both apps use Apple's official App Store Connect API and store credentials on-device. The differences are price (AppMeta Pulse is $0.99/month vs AppStats' $2.99/month, and $17.99 lifetime vs $49.99 lifetime) and data collection (AppMeta Pulse declares zero collection on its privacy label; AppStats' privacy label declares usage data and diagnostics).
How is AppMeta Pulse different from AppStats?
The two apps overlap heavily on core function. The substantive differences: (1) AppMeta Pulse is roughly a third the monthly price and 64% cheaper at the lifetime tier; (2) AppMeta Pulse's privacy label declares zero data collection while AppStats declares usage data and diagnostics; (3) AppStats has dedicated daily notifications, richer widget sizes, and a multi-currency toggle that AppMeta Pulse currently does not match. Pick AppMeta Pulse for price and zero tracking; stay with AppStats for notifications and widgets.
Does AppMeta Pulse have notifications or widgets like AppStats?
AppMeta Pulse focuses on the on-demand glance. AppStats has a more notification-and-widget-led product surface. If you check your numbers via morning notifications or rely on multiple widget sizes, AppStats is the better fit today.
Do both apps use the official App Store Connect API?
Yes. Both use Apple's official App Store Connect API with a user-generated .p8 API key stored in the system Keychain. Neither scrapes the web dashboard. The difference is in what each app does with the data after fetching it.
Is AppMeta Pulse cheaper than AppStats?
Monthly: $0.99 vs $2.99 (~67% cheaper). Yearly: $8.99 vs $19.99 (~55% cheaper). Lifetime: $17.99 vs $49.99 (~64% cheaper). Check each app's current App Store listing for trial details.
