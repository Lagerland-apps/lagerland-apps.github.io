How does Chessful compare to Chess.com?

Chess.com is a platform; Chessful is a training tool. Different categories. Here's where they overlap.

Where the engine runs
Chessful: Stockfish 18, locally, on every device the app runs on.

Chess.com: Cloud-based analysis on their servers, returned to the client.
Daily analysis quota
Chessful: None. Analyze as many games as you have CPU.

Chess.com: Free tier limits "Game Review" usage; full access via Premium.
What you get from a "mistake"
Chessful: Plain-language explanation plus an adaptive drill built from your real recurring weakness.

Chess.com: Strong move-quality classification, with coach-style narration in Premium tiers.
Privacy / account
Chessful: No account. Games stay on-device. See the Privacy Manifest.

Chess.com: Account required; game history lives in your profile.

Who should pick Chessful vs Chess.com?

Pick Chessful if…
  • You play on multiple platforms (Chess.com, Lichess, OTB) and want one analysis home
  • You analyze a lot of games and the daily quota frustrates you
  • You want analysis without an account or cloud dependency
  • You travel and play offline and want full-strength analysis on a plane
  • You want training built from your weaknesses, not generic puzzles
Stick with Chess.com if…
  • Playing live games against opponents is the primary need (Chessful is analysis-only)
  • You rely on Chess.com's social, clubs, daily puzzles, and Premium Game Review narration
  • You want video lessons from named masters
  • You want rated tournament structure and tactics ratings on the same platform

FAQ

What is the best private alternative to Chess.com analysis?
Chessful is a private, on-device alternative for analyzing your chess games. It runs Stockfish 18 locally on iPhone, iPad, and Mac, explains your mistakes in plain language, and builds adaptive training drills from your actual weaknesses — no account, no cloud, no daily analysis quota.
Does Chessful run Stockfish on-device?
Yes. Chessful uses Stockfish 18 compiled to run on iPhone, iPad, and Mac, so move-by-move evaluation happens entirely on your device. No game data is uploaded for analysis.
Can I import games from Chess.com or Lichess into Chessful?
Yes. Chessful imports games in standard PGN format, which both Chess.com and Lichess can export. Once imported, analysis and training are local.
Does Chessful require an account?
No. Chessful has no account system. Your games and training history stay on your device, with optional iCloud sync through your own iCloud account.
