How does Mockly compare to Picsew?

Different categories. Picsew is a phone-side stitcher. Mockly is a Mac-native App Store screenshot pipeline.

Platform
Mockly: Native Mac. Mouse, keyboard, multi-monitor.

Picsew: iPhone (and iPad). Touch-first.
Device frames
Mockly: Real 3D device meshes rendered in Metal. Angle, lighting, reflections.

Picsew: Flat device frames overlaid on screenshots.
Localisation
Mockly: Built-in AI translation for App Store localisations.

Picsew: Not the use case; you'd edit text per locale by hand.
App Store Connect upload
Mockly: Direct upload from the app.

Picsew: Export to Files / Photos; upload separately.

Who should pick Mockly vs Picsew?

Pick Mockly if…
  • You're producing App Store screenshots for a real release
  • You ship across multiple device sizes (iPhone, iPad, Mac)
  • You localise to more than one App Store region
  • You want a native Mac UI with the full keyboard, mouse, multi-monitor workflow
  • You'd rather pay once than subscribe
Stick with Picsew if…
  • You're stitching long screenshots on iPhone for social media or chat
  • You don't need 3D device frames or App Store-quality output
  • You work entirely on the phone with no Mac in the loop
  • Picsew's workflow already fits your screenshot needs

What is the best Mac alternative to Picsew for App Store screenshots?
Mockly is a native Mac alternative to Picsew built specifically for App Store screenshots. It renders real 3D device models in Metal at any output resolution, supports AI translation across locales, and uploads directly to App Store Connect — a workflow that's awkward to do from a phone-side stitching app.
Does Mockly render real 3D device models?
Yes. Mockly uses Apple's Metal framework to render real 3D device meshes — iPhone, iPad, and Mac — at the angle, lighting, and resolution you set. The output is true device-shaped, not a flat overlay.
Can Mockly translate screenshot text for App Store localisations?
Yes. Built-in AI translation generates localised variants of your screenshot text so you can ship the same screenshot set across multiple App Store locales in one pass.
Is Mockly a subscription?
No. Mockly is a one-time Mac App Store purchase — no recurring fee.
