When should I pick Jomo instead?

Honest answer — pick Jomo if any of these matter more than a movement-gated block:

  • You want one blocker across iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Jomo spans all three; EarnLock is iOS + Apple Watch only.
  • You prefer session- and schedule-based blocking with a strict mode over a single daily activity gate.
  • You like Jomo's in-app unlock actions (meditation and similar) and don't want movement as the cost.
  • Jomo's design and onboarding click with you — it's a genuinely polished app and that matters for a tool you'll see daily.

EarnLock is for the person who wants the unlock cost to be physical — a walk, a workout, calories burned — enforced by Apple's Family Controls, proven by Apple Health, with the count on their wrist, at a fraction of Jomo's lifetime price.

FAQ

What is the best alternative to Jomo?
EarnLock is a movement-based, privacy-first alternative to Jomo. Jomo blocks apps with scheduled sessions, a strict mode, and active actions to unlock. EarnLock shields your chosen apps through Apple's Family Controls until you hit a real daily activity goal — steps, active minutes, or active energy — measured by Apple Health, with a live progress ring on every blocked app. $19.99 lifetime (one-time) versus Jomo's roughly $99.99 lifetime. No account, no analytics SDK, no server.
How is EarnLock different from Jomo?
Jomo is session- and schedule-based with a strict mode and unlock actions like meditation. EarnLock's gate is specifically movement: the app stays shielded until your body hits a daily activity goal measured by Apple Health, and the shield shows a live progress ring counting down to unlock. EarnLock makes the Apple Watch a first-class surface. Jomo covers iPhone, iPad, and Mac; EarnLock is iOS and Apple Watch only. EarnLock requires no account and ships zero tracking SDKs.
Is EarnLock cheaper than Jomo?
Yes. EarnLock is $19.99 lifetime (one-time). Jomo is typically a subscription — around $5.99/month or $29.99/year — with a one-time option near $99.99. EarnLock's lifetime tier costs roughly a fifth of Jomo's lifetime price, and both apps have ways to start without paying. Re-verify current prices before switching.
Does Jomo work on Mac and iPad?
Yes — Jomo is designed for iPhone, iPad, and Mac, which is a genuine advantage if you want one blocker across all your Apple devices. EarnLock is iOS and Apple Watch only, because its mechanic depends on Apple's Family Controls and HealthKit on the iPhone and Watch. If cross-device coverage matters more than a movement gate, Jomo is the better fit.
Is EarnLock private?
Yes. EarnLock has no account, no server, no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, and no third-party SDKs of any kind. HealthKit data is read on-device only and never transmitted off the device. Apple's App Privacy nutrition label shows Data Not Collected for every release. Self-restriction only — never parental control.
Can EarnLock block the same apps Jomo can?
Yes. Both apps build on Apple's Family Controls framework, so the picker that selects which apps to block is the same iOS-provided UI. Any app or category Jomo can block, EarnLock can shield — the difference is that EarnLock keeps it locked until you move, and surfaces the count on your Apple Watch.
