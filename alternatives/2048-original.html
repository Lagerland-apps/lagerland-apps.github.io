FAQ

What is the original 2048, and how is Zen 2048 different?
The original 2048 was made by Gabriele Cirulli in 2014 and open-sourced (MIT) as a free browser game. Zen 2048 keeps its rules exactly but rebuilds it as a native iPhone app — a real 3D board you can tilt, 17 designer themes, haptics, a daily challenge, and offline play.
Can I still play the original 2048 for free?
Yes — it's free and open-source, still playable in a browser. But it's a web page: no real offline mode, a flat board, no themes or haptics. Zen 2048 is also free, but native and built for touch.
Is Zen 2048 just another 2048 clone?
Every 2048 shares the open-source mechanic. Most clones differ by adding ads and stores; Zen 2048 differs by adding craft — a genuinely 3D board, designer themes, haptics — with no ads, no tracking, and no account.
Does Zen 2048 work offline like a real app?
Yes, fully. Everything runs on-device with no connection required; scores, best tile, streak, and themes are stored locally. It only touches the network for optional purchases or Game Center.
Is Zen 2048 free with no ads?
Yes — the whole game is free with no ads and no tracking. Only optional theme packs ($1.99-$7.99) and undo tokens ($0.99-$6.99) cost anything, and neither is required.
