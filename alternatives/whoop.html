FAQ

What is the best Whoop alternative without a subscription or band?
Observa. It reads your Apple Health data — HRV, RHR, sleep — and interprets which habits move each metric against your personal baseline, on-device, no required subscription for the core. It uses whatever feeds Apple Health: your Apple Watch, or metrics Whoop shares there. Runs on iPhone and iPad.
Do I need an Apple Watch, or can Observa use my Whoop data?
No Apple Watch needed. Observa reads Apple Health, and Whoop can share sleep and workout data there from its own app. Whoop's most granular signals stay in Whoop's app, so keep Whoop for those; for plain-English interpretation against your baseline, Observa works from your Apple Health data on iPhone and iPad.
Can Observa replace my Whoop band?
It depends. Whoop's hardware captures granular data an Apple Watch doesn't. Observa adds no sensors; it makes the data already in Apple Health meaningful with plain-English correlations and a personal baseline. Want interpretation without a second subscription? Observa fits, working from Apple Watch or Whoop-shared data.
Is Observa cheaper than Whoop?
Yes — Whoop is ~$30/month membership including hardware. Observa is free at core, Pro from $3.99/month or $49.99 lifetime, using the Apple Watch you already own.
Is Observa more private than Whoop?
Yes — entirely on-device, no account, no analytics; App Privacy label lists zero data collected. Whoop is account- and cloud-based.
Does Observa give a recovery score like Whoop?
Not a single proprietary 0–100 number. Observa scores each metric against your 60-day baseline and tells you what's moving it, with confidence. Want a single percentage? Whoop. Want the reasoning? Observa.
Does Observa need any extra hardware?
No — it reads whatever is already in your Apple Health, from an Apple Watch, a Whoop sharing to Health, or another device. Runs on iPhone and iPad.
