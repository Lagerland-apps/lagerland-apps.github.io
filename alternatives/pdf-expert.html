FAQ

Is MediaKit a good PDF Expert alternative?
It depends. PDF Expert is a deep, dedicated PDF editor — for serious annotation, text and page editing, form filling, and cross-device sync it's the stronger tool and the better pick. MediaKit is an on-device PDF utility (compress, merge, split, redact, OCR, sign) bundled inside a 152-tool media app. If your PDF work is occasional and you also convert video, audio, and images, MediaKit is a practical alternative with a one-time price.
Can MediaKit edit PDFs like PDF Expert?
Not to the same depth. PDF Expert is built for rich PDF editing and annotation. MediaKit covers utility operations — merge, split, compress, encrypt, redact, sign, OCR, watermark, page numbers, and compare — via Apple's PDFKit and Vision. For heavy editing, PDF Expert is the better choice; for quick on-device PDF tasks, MediaKit handles them without a subscription.
Does MediaKit have a subscription like PDF Expert?
MediaKit offers a subscription but also a one-time option: Pro is $3.99/month, $22.99/year, or $39.99 once for lifetime. PDF Expert moved to a subscription model (around $79.99/year, with a one-time lifetime option around $139.99) plus a limited free tier. To avoid recurring billing for PDF utility work, MediaKit's $39.99 lifetime is a one-time purchase.
Does MediaKit upload my PDFs like a cloud service?
No. Every MediaKit operation runs 100% on-device via Apple frameworks — no upload, no account, no tracking. PDF Expert also edits locally, with optional cloud sync and AI features that may use the network. MediaKit keeps everything on your iPhone or Mac.
Does MediaKit work on iPhone and Mac like PDF Expert?
Yes — MediaKit is a Universal Purchase: buy once, use on both iPhone and Mac signed in with the same Apple ID. PDF Expert covers iPhone, iPad, and Mac through its subscription. MediaKit currently targets iPhone and Mac.
When should I pick PDF Expert instead of MediaKit?
Pick PDF Expert if PDFs are central to your work and you need deep annotation, advanced text and page editing, form filling at scale, and sync across iPhone, iPad, and Mac — it's a more powerful dedicated editor. Pick MediaKit if your PDF needs are occasional utility tasks and you also want on-device video, audio, image, and archive tools in one app without a subscription.
