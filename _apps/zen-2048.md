---
Zen 2048 is a [free, ad-free 2048 puzzle for iPhone](#features) rebuilt as a real 3D board. It keeps the rules everyone already knows — [swipe to merge equal tiles, chase the next power of two](#how-it-works) — and changes everything about how the game feels: the playfield is rendered in true 3D, so you can tilt and spin it to any angle and watch the tiles catch the light, and every tile is a designed material rather than a flat colour.

The reason it exists is simple. [2048 was open-sourced by Gabriele Cirulli in 2014](/alternatives/2048-original/) and promptly buried under a decade of ads, lives, and cosmetic stores on the App Store. Zen 2048 puts the puzzle back at the centre: [no ads, no timers, no account, and nothing tracked](/apps/zen-2048/privacy/) — the App Store privacy label shows nothing collected, because nothing is. It's free to play, top to bottom.

What you can buy is optional and only ever cosmetic or convenience. There are [17 designer themes across seven material families](#themes) — glass, ceramic, paper, metal, neon, pastel, and monochrome — from Amber and Glacier Ice to Midnight Neon, Sakura Pastel, and Aurora Drift. The Classic board is free; the other sixteen come in four packs ($1.99–$2.99 each), or all of them plus every future pack in the [Complete Collection for $7.99](/apps/zen-2048/#pricing). Undo tokens ($0.99–$6.99) are a safety net you can buy if you like, never a wall.

Around the board there's a [fresh daily challenge, streaks, best-score and best-tile tracking, and optional Game Center achievements](#features) — small reasons to come back, none of them designed to guilt-trip you. Everything runs on your device. The trade-off is that Zen 2048 is single-player and built for iPhone today; if you want a brand-new mechanic, [Threes](/alternatives/threes/) is the classic to buy, and if you want the ad-supported mass-market version, that's [2048 by Ketchapp](/alternatives/2048-ketchapp/) — the [side-by-side comparisons](#compare-heading-zen-2048) are honest about exactly where each one fits.

Free to play, no ads, no tracking, no account — published by Lagerland Apps, an independent Apple developer in Finland. The design story is in the journal: [how we made 2048 a real 3D board](/journal/making-2048-a-real-3d-board/), and [why a calm, ad-free 2048 still belongs on the App Store](/journal/a-2048-game-for-an-attentional-cease-fire/). For the wider category, see [the best 2048 apps for iPhone](/guides/best-2048-apps-iphone/).
